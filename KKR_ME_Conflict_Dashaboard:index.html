Crisis Overview & Strategic Implications for KKR
The Catalyst Feb 28, 2026

The initiation of Operation Epic Fury on February 28, 2026, and the subsequent de facto closure of the Strait of Hormuz represent the most severe global energy and supply chain dislocation since the 1970s. As of the anchor date, March 28, 2026, global markets are processing an unprecedented physical supply shock, with approximately 15.8 million barrels per day of crude oil and 20% of global liquefied natural gas stranded in the Persian Gulf. Brent crude has surged to $112.57 per barrel, fundamentally altering the macroeconomic assumptions underpinning private equity underwriting, capital formation, and portfolio operations.

Implications for KKR's $744B Platform Firm-Wide

For Kohlberg Kravis Roberts & Co., the implications span the entirety of the firm's $744 billion asset under management footprint. The most acute portfolio risks reside in industrial platforms reliant on Middle Eastern petrochemical feedstocks, aviation assets facing jet fuel price shocks, and physical infrastructure investments located within the Gulf Cooperation Council. Specifically, KKR's minority stake in Abu Dhabi National Oil Company Gas Pipeline Assets and regional data center joint ventures face direct kinetic and operational risks.

Margin compression in manufacturing and logistics platforms will be severe over the next two quarters where contractual pass-through protections remain weak.

Fundraising & Sovereign Capital Bifurcation LP Impact

The conflict threatens to bifurcate sovereign wealth fund behavior, carrying profound fundraising and investor implications. Middle Eastern capital providers, which manage over $4 trillion, are confronting daily revenue losses of approximately $1.1 billion due to the export blockade. This introduces the risk of delayed deployment, reprioritization toward domestic stabilization, and a shift away from blind-pool commitments toward direct co-investments. Conversely, non-Middle Eastern sovereigns and pensions face severe denominator effects as public equities reprice, though demand for high-quality, inflation-linked real assets is accelerating.
